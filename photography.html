Photography

“Photography has always been my way of capturing the world I see around me. Recently, I've become more interested in capturing skewed moments: the look on someone’s face, or lights on a tree branch making trails in the night sky - moments normally overlooked or even completely invisible to the naked eye.” - Aran Gilmore is a Boston artist, concentrating in photography. He’s been shooting for more than 20 years and is currently working on a Masters degree in digital media at Harvard University.

  • An image of rollerblading/rollerskating street performers in front of Notre Dame Cathedral, launching from a ramp to jump over a bar in ever increasing heights.

  • An image of an amusment park ride near the Louvre. It's taken at a long exposure, which allows the lights to trail in a circle as the arm of the ride swings around.
